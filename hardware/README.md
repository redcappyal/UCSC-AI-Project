# Fin mount — printable parts (v3: own phone clamp, old mast kept)

Generated by `generate_mount_stls.py` from the numbers in `../docs/mount-spec.md`
and the design doc (`../docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-23-mount-v2-clamp-redesign-design.md`,
v3 addendum):

```
python3 hardware/generate_mount_stls.py
```

**Zero purchased hardware, and the v1 architecture you already validated.**
The custom cradle is replaced by a phone clamp of our own design that borrows
the *mechanism* of the Printables tripod clamp — fixed jaw + rail-guided
sliding jaw + screw drive — built entirely from our proven printed M12×2.5
thread and the v1 dovetail joint family. It mounts through the same
female-dovetail back boss + tapered peg the v1 cradle used, onto the same
wedge/mast/saddle stack. **The mast is unchanged from v1 — do not reprint it**
(the emitted `mast.stl` is geometrically identical; verified 0 mm³ difference).

## v3 changes from the field test

- **Reprint: saddle, wedges, phone clamp parts. Reuse: mast, pins.**
- Wall-stop bosses / gap fillers **deleted** from the saddle — slide toward
  the court; screw friction holds. (Recalibrate after each remount.)
- Wedge-face rails rebuilt with **2 mm-embedded unions** — the v1 rails were
  corrupted by a 0.05 mm boolean overlap (root cause of the unmountable
  cradle). Also fixed: the v1 wedge peg bore was offset along the *slope*
  while the cradle's was offset along the *wall* — they never lined up. The
  bore pair is now derived from the stop-tab seat so it aligns by construction.
- Phone-size adjustability: the clamp's sliding jaw covers **68–95 mm** phone
  widths, 8–11 mm thick (45° jaw lips wedge thinner phones snug).
- Saddle screw ball tip + snap pad-cap replaced by a **threaded-on Ø18 tip
  disc** (CA-glue) with a glued TPU face pad — nothing can fall out. The same
  disc part captures the phone-jaw screw in its carriage (print 2).
- Every peg bore is modeled open and **asserted open** at generation time.
  The saddle-cap bore stays blind *by design* (a peg must never reach glass).
- Optics are computed and asserted per wedge: WSF court-plane margin ≥3 mm,
  and the spec §10 functional target — floor visible to ≤0.6 m of the back
  wall — currently 0.57 m (wedge A) / 0.47 m (wedge B) with the 48 mm mast.

## Assembly (one-time, tool-free + CA glue)

1. Glue TPU (CA): seat strip in the cap underside, jaw pads in the saddle
   channel, screw pad into one tip disc, phone-jaw strips into the top-hook
   and carriage pockets, back strips into the plate-front recesses.
2. Saddle screw: thread into the boss from outside; inside the channel,
   thread on the (padded) tip disc and glue.
3. Phone clamp: slide the carriage onto the body rail from the bottom; drop
   the bare tip disc into the carriage's coin slot; thread the jaw screw
   through the boss nut into the disc; glue the disc to the screw tip.
4. Slide the mast rail into the saddle-cap slot to the end; peg.
5. Slide the wedge onto the mast rail to the stop; peg.
6. Slide the clamp body's back boss onto the wedge face rail to the stop; peg
   (the bore enters from the plate front at the boss, x −1 / y 64).
7. On court: hook over the fin, slide toward the court, snug the thumbscrew.
   Phone in landscape, lens corner up: top edge under the fixed hook, then
   screw the bottom jaw closed.

Fit checks done in generation: all meshes watertight; every peg bore verified
open; body↔wedge and carriage↔body sectioned at assembly positions with 0 mm³
interference (carriage checked at both travel extremes); court-plane margin
and near-wall floor-visibility asserted for both wedges. Assembly render:
`../docs/figures/mount-v2-assembly.png`.

## Mechanism attribution

The screw-driven sliding-jaw layout is modeled on Stamos's "Tripod Mobile
Phone Clamp V2" (printables.com model 25969). No files from it are used —
the geometry here is original and parametric.
