# Fin mount — printable parts (v2, tripod-clamp revision)

Generated by `generate_mount_stls.py` from the numbers in `../docs/mount-spec.md`
and the v2 design doc (`../docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-23-mount-v2-clamp-redesign-design.md`):

```
python3 hardware/generate_mount_stls.py
```

**Zero purchased hardware.** v2 replaces the custom phone cradle with the
Printables **"Tripod Mobile Phone Clamp V2"** (screw-driven jaws → fits any
phone). Only the clamp's *lower* part is remixed: its tripod foot (which needs
a metal 1/4-20 nut in stock form) is swallowed by a grafted block carrying a
**T-slot** that slides onto a T-rail on the wedge face — end stop + tapered
peg, the same joint UX as the saddle/mast dovetails that passed the
2026-07-23 hardware test. Once assembled (3 pegs + glued pads + threaded-on
screw tip), the whole mount is **one connected part** for transport.

## v2 changes from the field test

- Wall-stop bosses / gap fillers **deleted** — slide the saddle as far toward
  the court as you like; the clamp screw's friction holds position.
- Wedge-face joint rebuilt as a **T-rail** with 2 mm-embedded unions (the v1
  face dovetails were corrupted by a 0.05 mm boolean overlap — root cause of
  the unmountable cradle).
- Wedge faces re-derived: the clamp bar mounts perpendicular to the face, so
  **face angle = optical pitch** (A 40°, B 32°) and the face descends toward
  the wall. Mast height and positions are **solved in the generator** against
  the WSF court-plane rule and the edge-occlusion ratio (spec §8) and asserted.
- Screw ball tip + snap pad-cap replaced by a **threaded-on Ø18 tip disc**
  (CA-glue it) — the TPU face pad can no longer fall out.
- Every peg bore is modeled open and **asserted open** at generation time
  (through wherever function allows; the saddle-cap bore is blind *by design*
  so a peg can never reach the glass). If a printed hole still arrives filled,
  check the slicer preview — the STLs are verified open.

## Why the mast is tall

With the phone held in a clamp bar (rather than lying on the wedge), the lens
sits ~45 mm outboard of the court-side wall face. The WSF edge-occlusion
requirement (spec §8: lens height / setback ≥ 4.92) then forces the lens
~265 mm above the wall edge → 160 mm column. The generator solves and asserts
this; if it's unwieldy on site, the knobs are `PW_MIN/PW_MAX` (measure the
actual phone — a narrower range buys height back) and the occlusion margin.

## Assembly (one-time, tool-free + CA glue)

1. Glue TPU pads (CA): seat strip in the cap underside, jaw pads in the
   channel faces, screw pad in the tip disc pocket.
2. Assemble the Printables clamp per its instructions (thread insert glued
   into the lower's boss, screw halves + knurl head, upper slides on).
3. Thread `clamp_screw` into the saddle boss from outside; inside the channel,
   thread `screw_tip_disc` onto the protruding tip and CA-glue it.
4. Slide the mast's base rail into the saddle-cap slot to the end; drop a peg.
5. Slide the wedge onto the mast rail to the stop; drop a peg (low side).
6. Slide the clamp's T-slot onto the wedge's T-rail to the stop; drop a peg
   through the block beside the bar.
7. On court: hook the saddle over the fin at the wall junction, slide toward
   the court as far as it goes, snug the thumbscrew; clamp the phone
   (landscape, lens corner up) with the knurled wheel.

Fit checks done in generation: all meshes watertight; every peg bore verified
open; T-joint sectioned at assembly position with designed clearances (0 mm³
interference); optics constraints (court-plane margin, occlusion ratio)
asserted for both wedges. Assembly render: `../docs/figures/mount-v2-assembly.png`.
